BEELINE Poetry Festival

BEELINE Poetry Festival is a hypothetical event held annually in New York City, NY, showcasing poetry readings, question-and-answer sessions, workshops, and more. BEELINE gets its name from the concept that what most recognizably differentiates poetry from prose is the use of the line to set pacing, divide phrases and emphasize words, as well as the idea that—like a beeline—poetry’s concision follows the most direct route.

BEELINE advertises digitally through their app, social media, and email blasts. Social media channels include Facebook and Instagram, where BEELINE posts poems as kinetic type to promote the annual chapbook, updates to the poet line-up, announcements, and more.

BEELINE's responsive, coded email blast announces there is only one month until the festival, and contains helpful information and a call-to-action to download BEELINE’s app.

BEELINE’s app contains features such as an interactive scheduling page, an online shop, information about attending poets, and general festival info.

BEELINE App

lucky by Millie Redd, kinetic type poem

Festival materials include a lanyard and name card given to each registered attendee, a t-shirt for the festival volunteers, and event signage outside of each room.

Each year, BEELINE publishes their annual festival chapbook—a small collection of poems based upon a central theme and selected from attendee submissions, featuring minimal illustrations and select pull-quotes.

2020 Chapbook, Tick Tock

2019 Chapbook, Braving the Cold

2018 Chapbook, In the Margins

Merchandise for the festival can be purchased online or at the event. Items include a 2020 Collector’s Poster; a tote bag; a pencil bag; a quote bookmark; and an Edgar Allan Poe poetry collection, which—when flipped over—doubles as a lined notebook.
